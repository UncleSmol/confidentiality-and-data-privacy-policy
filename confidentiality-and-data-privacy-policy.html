Confidentiality and Data Privacy Policy

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear guidelines on the protection of confidential and private information related to Villa Roma Hotel's guests, employees, and business operations. This policy helps ensure that all personal, financial, and operational data is handled securely and that Villa Roma remains compliant with data protection regulations.

Scope

This policy applies to all Villa Roma Hotel employees, contractors, trainees, and any third parties handling information on behalf of the hotel.

Policy Guidelines

Confidential Information Categories

  • Guest Information: Includes personal details (such as names, contact information, and ID numbers), booking history, payment information, and preferences.
  • Employee Information: Includes personal, financial, medical, and performance-related details.
  • Business Information: Covers proprietary information such as financial records, business strategies, vendor contracts, pricing details, and operational procedures.
  • Training Program Information: For trainees, this includes performance records, training evaluations, and attendance records.

Data Handling and Storage

  • Access Control: Only authorized personnel may access confidential information. Access is granted on a need-to-know basis, depending on job roles and responsibilities.
  • Digital Data Storage: All electronic records must be stored securely in password-protected systems. Sensitive data must be encrypted to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Physical Data Storage: Hard copies of confidential information must be stored in locked filing cabinets. Access to these physical files is restricted to authorized personnel.
  • Data Minimization: Employees should collect only the necessary information required to perform job duties or deliver services to guests. Unnecessary data collection is to be avoided.

Data Privacy Practices

  • Data Collection and Consent: Employees and trainees must inform guests of the purpose of collecting their data and obtain consent for its use in accordance with privacy laws.
  • Transparency with Guests: Guests should be informed about how their data is used, stored, and protected. Privacy notices are available at check-in or online.
  • Anonymization and Pseudonymization: Where possible, data should be anonymized or pseudonymized, particularly for analytical or statistical purposes.

Data Sharing and Disclosure

  • Third-Party Sharing: Confidential information must not be shared with third parties unless it is essential for service delivery or required by law. Third parties handling guest or employee information must comply with data protection standards.
  • Internal Sharing: Information shared internally must be limited to those directly involved in the related task or service, following the principle of minimum disclosure.
  • Legal Obligations: In cases where disclosure is required by law (e.g., law enforcement requests), it must be approved by management and conducted transparently.

Security and Protection Measures

  • Data Security Training: Employees and trainees must undergo regular data protection and cybersecurity training to understand best practices and compliance requirements.
  • Password Protection: All systems with access to confidential data require strong, unique passwords, which should be updated regularly.
  • Device Security: Employees must ensure all devices used for work purposes are secure. Devices should be locked when not in use, and data should not be stored on personal devices unless approved.
  • Incident Reporting: Any data breach, loss, or unauthorized access must be reported immediately to the IT or HR department. Prompt reporting is essential to contain and mitigate any potential risks.

Prohibited Actions

  • Unauthorized Access: Accessing, reviewing, or sharing confidential information without permission is strictly prohibited.
  • Personal Use: Confidential information must not be used for personal purposes or shared with unauthorized individuals, including friends or family.
  • Social Media and Public Disclosure: Employees and trainees must refrain from discussing any sensitive information about guests, colleagues, or business operations on social media or other public forums.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

  • Disciplinary Action: Unauthorized access, misuse, or disclosure of confidential information may result in disciplinary measures, including verbal or written warnings, suspension, or termination, depending on the severity of the breach.
  • Legal Ramifications: Non-compliance may result in legal action against the individual responsible, particularly in cases of gross negligence or malicious intent.

Policy Review and Updates

This Confidentiality and Data Privacy Policy will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to ensure continued compliance with industry standards and evolving data protection laws. Any updates will be communicated to all employees and trainees.

Acknowledgment

All employees and trainees must review and sign an acknowledgment of this policy as a condition of employment or participation in the training program, confirming their commitment to uphold confidentiality and data privacy standards.
